# Statement of Purpose - Krishna Aggarwal

I am a Class 12 student from Delhi with a strong interest in engineering, computing, and cybersecurity. My journey began not with formal labs or advanced software, but with curiosity about how systems work and how they can be improved through practical problem-solving.

In Class 10, I designed and built a Portable Automatic Washing Machine using basic mechanical and electrical components such as a motor, submersible pump, switches, and a power conversion setup. This project was selected for the INSPIRE MANAK Award 2023 by the Department of Science & Technology, Government of India, and I received a grant of ₹10,000. The project was later exhibited at the District/State Level Science Exhibition under the Directorate of Education, GNCT Delhi. This experience gave me confidence that I could independently build, test, and explain a working system.

Later, I led a team in EMC 2025 to develop an enhanced version of the same concept. That transition from solo building to team leadership taught me another important lesson: good systems are not only built with creativity, but also with structure, responsibility, and clear communication.

My interest in cybersecurity comes from the idea that the systems society depends on must not only function, but also remain secure, reliable, and resilient. As digital infrastructure becomes central to communication, governance, finance, and defence, cybersecurity is becoming one of the most important technical fields of the future. I want to contribute to that field by building strong foundations in programming, operating systems, networking, and secure system design.

To prepare myself, I completed a certificate course in C and C++ programming with Grade A and started building small programming projects, including cipher-based tools in C to understand basic cryptographic logic. I am also pursuing DCAP to improve my understanding of computer applications, networking, and operating system concepts. In addition, I developed my own HTML and CSS portfolio to present my work in a structured way.

I do not claim to be an advanced cybersecurity practitioner yet. At this stage, I see myself as a serious beginner with proven hands-on discipline, a record of building real projects, and a strong motivation to grow in secure computing. What I bring is not only interest, but evidence of persistence, self-learning, and execution.

I want to join this program because it matches both my current foundation and my future direction. I am ready to learn rigorously, strengthen my technical depth, and transition from basic project work to meaningful work in cybersecurity.

Krishna Aggarwal
