# Statement of Purpose
## IIT Kanpur B.Cybersecurity Program — 2026
## Krishna Aggarwal

---

I am Krishna Aggarwal, a Class 12 student from Sarvodaya Bal Vidyalaya, J&K Block, North-East Delhi. I am applying for the B.Cybersecurity program at IIT Kanpur because I have spent the last three years building real systems, finding their failure points, and wanting to understand those failures more deeply than school can teach me.

In Class 9, I built a portable automatic washing machine from first principles: a wooden chassis, an electric motor, a DC-to-AC inverter, a submersible pump, and a pulsator agitator. I did not follow a kit. I did not have a guide. I worked from the physics I understood and iterated until it functioned. The Department of Science & Technology, Government of India selected the project for the INSPIRE MANAK Award — a ₹10,000 cash prize — and it was subsequently exhibited at the District and State Level under the GNCT Delhi Science Branch in 2024.

The most important thing that project taught me had nothing to do with washing machines. It was this: every system has a condition where it breaks. The gap between "it works in the demo" and "it works under adversarial conditions" is where the real engineering happens. That is the definition of cybersecurity.

I completed a Certificate in C and C++ Programming with Grade A from ICE Computer Education, Sahibabad, and began writing cryptography programs: a Caesar cipher, a Vigenère cipher, an XOR cipher, and a password strength evaluator. I chose these projects deliberately. Implementing a Caesar cipher is not impressive. Understanding exactly why it is breakable by frequency analysis, and then implementing Vigenère to address that weakness, and then understanding why Vigenère is still broken by Kasiski examination — that is how you build actual intuition about what security means rather than what it looks like. I am currently pursuing a Diploma in Computer Applications and Programming (DCAP) from IICS Gokulpuri, which is building my foundation in networking protocols and operating system concepts.

In 2025, I led a 6-member team at the Engineering Model Competition with an enhanced version of my INSPIRE project, integrating UV-C germicidal irradiation. The most interesting engineering problem in that project was not the UV-C system itself — it was ensuring the UV lamp could not activate while the drum was accessible. That is a physical access control problem. It is the same category of problem as network segmentation, privilege escalation prevention, and secure boot: you are designing a system where the harmful capability is available but constrained by a trusted mechanism. I found that connection more interesting than the electrical engineering.

I know my JEE score does not reflect my abilities. It reflects one examination on one day, and I did not perform at my best. What does reflect my abilities is a government-recognised science award at age 14, a consistent record of building functional hardware without supervision, four working cryptography programs on GitHub, and this application — which I am writing because I am serious about this field and this program specifically.

IIT Kanpur's B.Cybersecurity program is the right place for me because it treats security as a foundational discipline, not an add-on to a computer science degree. I want to work on embedded systems security, hardware vulnerability research, and critical infrastructure protection. Those goals require exactly the kind of rigorous, first-principles education that IIT Kanpur provides.

I am not asking for a waiver on standards. I am asking for the chance to be evaluated on what I have actually built, not just on one entrance exam. Given that chance, I will work harder than anyone else in the program.
